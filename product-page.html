Luxury Cruising in the Galapagos Archipelago

The M/V Santa Cruz II offers guests 5 and 7 day Galapagos tours, exploring the Northern, Eastern and Western Galapagos Islands. Your highly-trained expedition leader will ensure that the outings fully explore the wonders of the Galapagos. Guides will adjust daily expeditions to the personal needs and interests of the guests, with activities ranging from land treks to aquatic activities.

The M/V Santa Cruz II began cruising the Galapagos Islands in October 2015. She guarantees that the utmost care will be given to the environment by implementing the newest sustainable advances in sailing, all while exploring in style and comfort. With five spacious decks and gorgeous chic interiors designed by the distinguished ship designer, Richard Nilsson, the Santa Cruz II offers tours that are better than ever.

Another Fun Heading

The Santa Cruz II has 50 modern cabins and can host up to 90 guests. There are many cabin options onboard that guests can choose from. A variety of single, double and family cabins are available. For the ultra-luxe tastes, the Santa Cruz offers the exclusive Darwin Suites which meet an exceptional standard of accommodation on the boat’s Panorama Deck. These suites provide guests with enhanced service, as well as particularly selected details such as a take-home Galapagos coffee table book and a tablet loan for each cabin.

The menu onboard is prepared by a Le Cordon Bleu chef, and consists of a mix of Ecuadorian and international cuisine. Most food is sourced locally, meaning freshly caught fish and seafood and organically grown fresh vegetables and fruit, all prepared by a passionate team. Meals are served in the Beagle Dining Room and snacks are served after expeditions, whether in the bar, in the library or on the outdoor terrace, where you can take in the fabulous vistas and marine breezes of the Galapagos. A 24-hour coffee station located in the library provides free coffee, tea, and water.

Northern Islands Route

Cruise aboard the Santa Cruz II yacht to the glorious white beach of Las Bachas, Santiago Island (Buccaneer Cove and Puerto Egas), Rabida, Bartolome Island, and the sunken crater of Genovesa Island, Galapagos’ best natural aviary. End the trip on a high note watching Galapagos giant tortoises stroll through their natural habitat in the highlands of Santa Cruz Island before heading to the airport at Baltra. This itinerary visits the following islands: Baltra, Santa Cruz, Santiago, Rabida, Bartolome, Genovesa and Baltra

Southern Islands Route

This journey starts and ends in Baltra. It takes you to the oldest islands in the Galapagos Archipelago. You will visit the sea lions of Mosquera Islet before heading to see the red-footed boobies in San Cristobal, the island where Darwin first set foot in the Galapagos.

Meet the endemic land iguanas of Santa Fe, observe the colours of South Plaza, and stop in at the world-famous Charles Darwin Research Station on Santa Cruz, before heading on to top-rated Española and North Seymour, with an in-between stop at Eden Islet. This itinerary visits the following islands: Baltra, Mosquera, San Cristobal, Santa Fe, South Plaza, Santa Cruz, Española and North Seymour

Accomodations

There are four room categories in total spread across three decks: Suite, Upper Suite, Corner Suite and Owner’s Suite. On the first deck there are 8 Suites complete with floor-to-ceiling panoramic windows, as well as 2 spacious Corner Suites facing the front of the ship. The second deck is comprised of 10 Upper Suites and the largest suite of all – the Owner’s Suite.

All suites feature all-natural bath amenities, a nightly turn down service, in-room electronic safe, and individual climate control.
